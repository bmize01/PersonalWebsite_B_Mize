Poetry

The Tick and The Tock of a Clock

I watch the hands move slowly,
wondering why anyone
would ever want to
create such a concept of time.
It’s always existed.
Maybe, just an invention.
A concept to everyone.
As sure as the sun
that rises every morning
until it sets in the west.
As the moon passes through phases
grabbing the tide in then
steadily pushing it away.
Years go by, minutes tick, hours pass
here I am stuck frozen
trying to find my time
to bloom
to grow
to live
to learn
to understand.
My season
My journey.
Will the time ever come for me?
As it waits for none.

Brighter Days

Misunderstood as the crow,
the black sheep,
the ugly duckling
wanting to be seen
to feel important
not taking up space
or bring shame.
A heavy cloud sits on the back
of all who are
Blessed are the outcasts
that haven't gotten discouraged;
who still hope the sun
will come out one day.

 

What A Waste


Reuse old love letters,
old leftover art magazines
destined to rot.
Scraps of paper,
shreds from your
junk mail,
abandoned anyways.
Old Calendars
filled with pretty art,
dates you’ve already forgotten
never to be seen again.
Free newspapers
left in front of hip
coffee shops.
Our supply endless.
We swim in the resource,
but why don’t we soak it instead?
Turn it into a new love note,
a new art piece.
Glue our hearts to the page.
It has the potential to have
more lives than kitty cats,
but why do we only want to give it one?
